      How to play Murdoku

      The setup

      The 9×9 grid is a house. Each suspect stood in exactly one cell at the moment of the murder. The victim 🪦 is one of them.

      The rules

      • Unique row + column. No two suspects share a row or a column. Everyone stands on their own row and their own column.
      • Killer alone with the victim. The killer 🔪 stood in the same room as the victim. Every other suspect was in a different room.

      The clues

      Each suspect has a clue describing where they stood. Clues use relative language:

      • Furniture references: "at the orchid table", "on the rug", "at the keys of a piano".
      • Other suspects: "in the same room as Crowe", "to the left of the piano", "directly below the lantern".
      • Room references: "in the only room with no furniture", "on the shop floor".

      How to play

      1. Click a suspect to read their clue.
      2. Drag, or click cell, to place them where you think they stood.
      3. Re-placing a suspect moves them; each suspect can only be in one cell.
      4. Identify who shared the room with the victim and mark them with 🔪 Mark as killer in the clue bubble.
      5. When every suspect is placed and a killer is named, Check solution unlocks. Click it.

      Toggles above the grid: hide room names, fade portraits so you can read the tile under them.
